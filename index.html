Manu Ginobili

Is he the most important Argentine athlete of all times?

The main purpose of this Tribute page is to find out if there is any other athlete in Argentina that might be greater than Manu Ginobili. You might say Maradona was the greatest, because he won the World Cup. But you must bear in mind that Basketball wasn't popular in Argentina until the "Golden Generation". So, Ginobili is in some way Maradona but he has been considered an all-star in the most competitive league and coming from a country where Basketball wasn't even the top 3 in popularity.

Career history

  • 1995–1996 Andino Sport Club
  • 1996–1998 Estudiantes de Bahía Blanca
  • 1998–2000 Viola Reggio Calabria
  • 2000–2002 Virtus Bologna
  • 2002–2018 San Antonio Spurs

Career highlights

  • 4× NBA champion (2003, 2005, 2007, 2014)
  • 2× NBA All-Star (2005, 2011)
  • 2× All-NBA Third Team (2008, 2011)
  • NBA Sixth Man of the Year (2008)
  • NBA All-Rookie Second Team (2003)
  • No. 20 retired by San Antonio Spurs
  • EuroLeague champion (2001)
  • EuroLeague Finals MVP (2001)
  • 2× EuroLeague Finals Top Scorer (2001, 2002)
  • All-EuroLeague First Team (2002)
  • Italian League champion (2001)
  • Italian Cup MVP (2002)
  • 2× Italian League MVP (2001, 2002)
  • 2× Olimpia de Oro (2003, 2004)
  • Diamond Konex Award (2010)
  • 50 Greatest EuroLeague Contributors (2008)
  • FIBA AmeriCup MVP (2001)
